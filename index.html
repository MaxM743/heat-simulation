Heat equation

I/ Formal definition

The two-dimensional heat equation is defined as :


$$\dfrac{\partial T}{\partial t}=D\left[\dfrac{\partial^2T}{\partial x^2}+\dfrac{\partial^2T}{\partial y^2}\right]$$

where $D$ is the thermal diffusivity and depends on the material's properties.

This equation describes how heat flows through a wall by diffusion, at a given point $(x, y)$.

II/ Finite Difference

First, we discretize the domain into a $N\times M$ grid of points. Hence we can rewrite the heat equation: $$\dfrac{\partial T_{n,m}^{t}}{\partial t}=D\left[\dfrac{\partial^2T_{n,m}^{t}}{\partial x^2}+\dfrac{\partial^2T_{n,m}^{t}}{\partial y^2}\right]$$ approximate the derivatives using the following approximation :


$$T_{n,m}^{t+1}=T_{n, m}^t + \alpha \left[T_{n, m-1}^t+T_{n, m+1}^t-2T_{n, m}^t\right]+\beta \left[T_{n-1,m}^t+T_{n+1, m}^t-2T_{n, m}^t\right]$$

where :


$$\alpha=D\dfrac{\Delta t}{\Delta x^2} \text{ et } \beta=D\dfrac{\Delta t}{\Delta y^2}$$
